Get Involved!

For 56 years, Wilson Field has been an icon. This green expanse has played host to glory, and not just for athletes. It is branded into the memory of every Wilson student. Of every visiting team. Of every member of the community who's decided to stop by for a crisp fall night under the lights. No stretch of grass means more to Southwest Portland.

But icons come at a cost. Maintaining a beautiful field under Oregon skies means tireless maintenance. It means miles of chalk lines, reservoirs of water, barrels of gasoline, hours of meticulous labor. It means fighting back 12 months of mud and weeds. It means Wilson Field can't fulfill it's intended purpose as a true community space. It means limited use in the fall and spring, and full closure during the winter.

We can fix this. We can make Wilson's fields what they were intended to be, but we need your help. With our community rallying around us, we can break ground on new neighborhood icons. The Wilson Fields Improvement Project has already been promised $77,000 in seed money. We need our community to do the rest. Estimates for remaking Wilson's fields stand between $1 million to $1.6 million. This cost includes a renovated entry gate, concession stand and field turf for additional practice fields. It will also improve our parking lot, bringing it up to city code.

We've set an ambitious goal of breaking ground one year from now. Fund raising starts now. Over the next 12 months, we'll reach out to local businesses and community leaders, to Trojan alumni and the families of future students. This fund raising campaign will take hard work. It'll take a community to make it happen. Icons usually do.
