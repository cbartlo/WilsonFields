Southwest Portland Post- October 2012

Wilson High School principal shares plan to replace stadium turf

The new Wilson High School principal shared the plan for the school stadium with members of the Hillsdale Neighborhood Association at its Sept. 5 meeting. Principal Brian Chatard said the plan is to replace the grass in the stadium with synthetic, all-weather turf. Having turf instead of grass would open up the field to winter sports, be low-maintenance and conserve water, Chatard said. The turf would last 10 years.

The Oregonian, October 4, 2012

Prep football: Wilson community pushing for artificial turf field

Wilson is the latest high school in Portland Public Schools to begin a community fundraising effort to install artificial turf on its football field. The Wilson Fields Improvement Project began in July with the goal of raising $1.2 million to build a field by the fall of 2013.
